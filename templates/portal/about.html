A little about

The U.S. Opiate Portal

The Opioid Epidemic is a current and expanding problem throughout the United States. Currently California has the most opioid related deaths with over 4,521 cases reported. Data shows that in 2018, a daily average of 192 people died from a drug overdose. 132 of those daily deaths were opioid related, which adds up to approximately 68% of the deaths. That ends up being approximately 48,200 deaths a year due to the use of Opioids. Clearly, this is a major issue we are facing and looking to solve in the present day.

In total, there are three main reasons given by the CDC to explain the rise in Opioid overdose deaths over recent years. The first reason is due to increased levels of Opioid prescriptions. The second reason is the use of Heroine. The last reason is attributed to a market for Synthetic Opioids such as Fentanyl (a mixture of heroin, counterfeit pills, and cocaine).

At the present time, there is no database or website accessible to the public, government, or medical field to see specific information about how many Opioids are being prescribed and who is prescribing on a national level. This is why we have created this website. As a team, we feel that if more prescribers of Opioids could know this information, they would be able to become more aware of how they are prescribing Opioids and be able to help their patients become more accountable with how they are using their prescriptions. We also feel this will help lead to less overdose deaths of Heroine because often Heroin addiction occurs from the use of another opioid beforehand.

Our Team has constructed this web application with many features to share information about the Opioid Epidemic to government officials and prescribers. We created a database that includes information about prescribers, states, drugs, and prescriptions. On our website you can search and see a list of current prescribers. You can add, delete, and edit these prescribers. You are also able to see a list of the drugs each prescriber is prescribing and if they are prescribing on average more Opioids than other doctors. Our website also contains a record of drugs commonly prescribed, lists if they are an Opioid, and shows the top ten prescribers who are prescribing that drug.
